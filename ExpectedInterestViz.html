Expected Interest In Self vs Success In Achieving A Date


This plot shows average success in dating on a 0-1 scale,
with expected interest in self from others in percentages.
The percentages exceed 100 because some subjects reported
expecting that more people than the pool of potentials held
would be interested in them. The assumption used here is that
the subjects had access to accurate information about that
pool, and that overprojecting was simply a mental slip that
revealed underlying excessive confidence. There were five
such data points - enough to add meaning and not to drop as
outliers. No correlation was found between expected interest
and success in getting a date, detracting from the hypothesis
that a positive mindset brings positive results.

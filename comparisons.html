Results Comparisons

This analysis uses a speed dating dataset to explore four factors
that could have an influence on whether a participant gets a date
at the end of the experiment:
  • Subject's expectation of potential dates' interest in subject
  • Subject's normal frequency of dating
  • Subject's goal for participating in speed dating
  • Subject's anticipated happiness with the pool of potential dates
This study found a significant correlation between an initial goal of
forming a serious relationship during speed dating and achieving a date
as an end result, as opposed to five other possible goals. No significant
relationship was found between the other three factors and achieving
a date.



Conclusions

Mindset, intentions and familiarity with dating are all plausible
influences on dating success. This analysis concludes that neither
having a positive mindset nor having familiarity with dating
impacts likelihood of getting a date. The key factor appears to
be setting concrete intentions. Limitations of this study include
including only heterosexuals, and a heavy reliance on undergrads
for a subject pool.
